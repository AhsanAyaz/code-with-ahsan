## Introduction

The following tips have been a result of 8 years+ experience in the Software Industry, working through several roles in startups, corporates, in-office, and remote. There might be a lot more tips that you folks have and I'd love to see them in the comments either on this post, or in the comments of my video

## Mistakes to avoid

- [Thinking your way is always the 'Best' way](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZNZu1Wlm7_E&t=36s)
- [Not reading the Documentation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZNZu1Wlm7_E&t=112s)
- [Being obsessed with Tools and Frameworks](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZNZu1Wlm7_E&t=166s)
- [Not focusing on your personal well-being](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZNZu1Wlm7_E&t=232s)
- [Following requirements blindly](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZNZu1Wlm7_E&t=323s)
- [Not shipping stuff until it is 'Perfect'](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZNZu1Wlm7_E&t=401s)
- [Not reaching out for help](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZNZu1Wlm7_E&t=472s)
- [Thinking your code will 'Always' be utilized](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZNZu1Wlm7_E&t=613s)
- [Not adding value when you see an opportunity](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZNZu1Wlm7_E&t=691s)
- [Not helping others grow. Especially your juniors](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZNZu1Wlm7_E&t=735s)
